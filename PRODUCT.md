# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective enterprise buyers, security-conscious decision-makers, and partners landing on
Vault's homepage. They arrive evaluating credibility in seconds. Context: a quick first
impression that must read as serious, global, and trustworthy before any copy is read.

## Product Purpose

A single-page brand hero for Vault, a fictional cybersecurity company. The page exists to
establish presence and gravitas — name, promise, and a sense of worldwide reach — not to
explain features or convert via a form. Success is an immediate impression of calm
competence and global scale.

## Brand Personality

Serious, precise, quietly confident. Voice is spare and declarative ("Protecting what
matters most."). Monochrome restraint with a single warm signal (orange globe markers)
standing in for active protection across the world.

## Anti-references

- SaaS-cream landing pages with gradient-metric hero templates.
- Neon "cyber" clichés: matrix rain, glitch type, lock-icon grids, hacker-green on black.
- Busy multi-section marketing scroll. This is one fold, one idea.

## Design Principles

- **One idea per viewport.** Name, promise, and the globe — nothing competes.
- **Restraint signals trust.** Near-monochrome; color is a single deliberate accent.
- **The globe is the argument.** Reach and vigilance shown, not stated.
- **Motion with intent.** A single orchestrated entrance and continuous rotation; no
  scattered micro-interactions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Light-only theme; body/title contrast verified against the panel background.
- Every animation has a `prefers-reduced-motion` path (entrance becomes an instant reveal).
- The globe is decorative (`aria-hidden`); no information is conveyed by it alone.
